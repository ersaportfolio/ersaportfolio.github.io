Halal Food

  • Date

    July - August 2023

  • Services

    Research with Design Thinking Method

  • Project Form

    Final Project

Halal Food application serves as a central hub of information for certified and non-certified halal food. Additionally, this application provides a platform for those involved in the halal product certification process in Indonesia. Halal certifying bodies (PPH) can access the application to submit information about the halal status of reviewed products to this central information website. Naturally, this information will be accessible to the entire community in Indonesia.

In the design of the Indonesian Halal Food application, the methodology employed in this study is based on the Design Thinking approach. This method consists of five stages, which include empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and testing. For testing, the researcher utilized the Single Ease Question (SEQ) method, System Usability Scale (SUS), and Net Promoter Score (NPS). Following this, the research can be summarized.

üß† Empathize

The first stage involves approaching the users to understand the issues experienced by the community regarding halal food. I conducted interviews and surveys, which resulted in a user empathy map, user pain and gain insights. These resources can be utilized by researchers to facilitate problem mapping.

üìë Define

After obtaining information about the issues, the next step is to classify them in order to simplify and narrow down several categories for resolution. In this stage, I will employ various methods of problem statement analysis. The artifacts generated in this phase include user personas, customer journey maps, and problem statements.

üí° Ideate

The next step is to generate ideas or solutions to address the classified problems. In the ideation stage, I gather ideas through brainstorming sessions, involving a team discussion to collectively come up with solutions and ideas that can address the user's issues.

After collecting and classifying the ideas, and conducting a voting process for the ideas and solutions, the next step is to map the ideas into an impact-effort matrix. Following this, we create the information architecture and develop the user task flow.

üñºÔ∏è Prototyping

Next, I will implement the solutions into the design and present the ideas or solutions in the form of the obtained prototype. In this stage, I create a Design System to ensure design consistency and develop low-fidelity to high-fidelity designs, along with their interactions, in the form of prototypes.

üìù Testing

From the designed prototype, testing will be conducted to gather feedback from users and evaluate the implemented solutions against the designed interface. Three testing methods will be used: SUS, SEQ, and NPS.
